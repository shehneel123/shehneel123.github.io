Washing and Drying Clothes

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

  1. Take the dirty clothes and hold them under running water until they are completely soaked. Or, you can dump them in a container full of water and have them soak in there.

    2. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  2. Take a bar of soap, and lifting the article of clothing, scrub at it with the soap till it begins to lather.

    3. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  3. Then gather the clothing in your hands, and scrub the material together. If you are aiming to wash a specific area, then focus on that. If not, it does not matter where you scrub.

    4. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  4. Once you think you are done, hold the clothing underneath the water and rinse it free of the soap. Or, you can submerge it in the container of water and scrub it free of soap.

    5. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  5. Afterwards, hang the wet clothing on a line or a place where it can hold the clothing. A spot in clear view of the sun is preferable if you want it to dry quicker.

    6. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

Sewing a Hole in Clothing

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

  1. Get a thread roll and a needle.

    Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  2. Unravel a long piece of thread from your needle. It should be more than you need.

    3. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  3. Rip out the length of the thread and stick it through the hole at the top of your needle.

    4. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  4. Hold both ends of the needle, and at the top of the thread, make a circle and loop through it to create a knot.

    5. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  5. Take your torn clothing and flip it inside out. Identify the hole, and bring your needle to the edge of the hole.

    6. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  6. Pierce through the bottom edge with your needle, and go through until you pierce through the fabric again in a straight line.

    7. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  7. Now, with this edge, make another hole and bring it to the bottom with your needle and thread.

    8. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  8. Continue this motion until your at the other side of the hole.

    9. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  9. Make a circle with the thread and loop it through with the needle. Knot it, and snip the excess thread.

    10. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  10. You just sewed your own hole!

    11. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

Knitting

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

  1. Begin by holding your knitting needle with the slip knot in your right hand. Grasp the working yarn (ball end) in your left hand and grasp it with your fingers. Place your thumb over the yarn.

    2. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  2. Bring your thumb down to loop the yarn around your thumb and bring your hand back up to an upright position.

    3. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  3. With your knitting needle in your right hand insert it below the yarn at the base of your thumb using an upward motion.

    4. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  4. Then just remove your thumb leaving the new loop on the knitting needle.

    5. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  5. When the loop is on the knitting needle just gently pull it until it is snug on the needle - not too tight.

    6. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  6. Continue until you cast on the amount of stitches you need.

    7. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  7. Hold your knitting needle with the cast on stitches in your left hand. Make sure that your working yarn is at the back of your work. Insert the right needle into the first loop on the left hand needle. Push the needle through from front to back.

    8. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  8. Then with your working yarn, wrap it counterclockwise around right hand needle bringing it in between both needles. Snug it up a little bit.

    9. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  9. With the yarn still wrapped around the right needle, bring right knitting needle through that first loop on the left knitting needle to complete the newly formed stitch. Keep pulling that newly formed stitch through the loop on the left needle.

    10. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  10. Slowly drop the first stitch off the left knitting needle. It has now been worked.

    11. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  11. Continue on until you have worked all the stitches on your knitting needle.

    12. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

Making a Fire: With Sticks

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

  1. Gather dry sticks together

    2. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  2. Grab two dry sticks. Place one stick on the ground, then hold the other over it vertically. Bring the vertical stick down until it is roughly on top of the stick on the ground.

    3. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  3. Place your palms on either side of the stick and vigorously move your hands back and forth so that the stick is spinning between your hands.

    4. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  4. Continue this motion until the friction builds up.

    5. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  5. Eventually, the stick on the ground will start smoking, and shortly after, a fire will erupt.

    6. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

Making a Fire: With Matches

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

  1. Take the box and pull out the section with the matches.

    2. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  2. Grab one of the matches, then push the section back in.

    3. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  3. With the part of the match that has the color, swipe it against the part of the box with the rough texture in a swift motion.

    4. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

  4. At this, a fire should erupt at the end of the match, but if it does not, continue to swipe at it until a fire occurs.

    5. Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

First Aid: Nose Bleed

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

Lean slightly forward and pinch your nose just below the bridge, where the cartilage and the bone come together. Maintain the pressure for 5 to 15 minutes. DO NOT lean back because you may swallow the blood and it can end up in your lungs.

Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

First Aid: Burns

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

Place the burn underneath cold running water. If there is no running water, wet a cloth and place it over the burn for fifteen minutes. After that, the burn should have cooled down, and it should heal on its own. DO NOT expose it to infectious settings, and keep it covered.

Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.

First Aid: Open Wounds

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

If you have a cut, make sure to wash it with water and cut so that it does not obtain infections. After, cover it with bandage if you have any, or cover it with long sleeved clothing.

Gardening

Memarsa Mushakil Al-Riadhyat

The soil should be healthy. Dampen the soil. Dig a hole about a few inches deep and place a few seedlings of your desired plant inside of it. Fill in the hole with more soil. Water the area daily, and eventually, a green plant will burst through the soil. Keep watering it.

Da farshat al-asnan dakhel femk wali al-jaza alaoui minn asnank safalia.
