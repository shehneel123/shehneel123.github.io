Grammar Rules

Al-Qawaaad Al-Nahwiya

Nouns are words that refer to a person, place, or thing.

Al-nufat he kelmat cheer elly shakhs oa mccann oa shaya.

Person:That lady is carrying a toy. Yasmine is eating.
Place: America is a big country.
Thing: That tent is weak. The chair is big.

Adjectives are words you use to describe nouns or pronouns.

Al-naout he kelmat testakhedmaha lowsef al-asmaa oa al-dimair.


The yellow kite flew off into the blue sky.

Adverbs are used with verbs, adjectives and other adverbs. They answer the questions how, when or where. Many adverbs end in -ly, but that is not always the case.

Wetstechdem al-zarouf ma al-afal walsevat veogeerha minn al-zarouf. Yagebon ola al-asailah keef wemte oa ayn. Al-adid minn al-zarouf tenthi fe -ly, wilkin hudha les ho al-hal daema.


Melissa hugged her new bear tightly and refused to let it go. (How did she hug? - tightly)

When dealing with the question of whether you should use who or that, remember 'who' refers to people and 'that' refers to things or groups.
Rachel is the one who called the police.
I don't like being in buildings that smell like smoke.

And el-tamel ma masala ma idha can yegeb alik estekhdam minn oa zelke tudkar "minn" yesher elly al-nas wa 'an' yesher elly al-ashia oa mejemouat.

Periods go at the end of complete sentences that are statements and after indirect questions.

Fatrat tuthap fe nahaya el-gamal al-kamila alti he al-bayanat vabaad al-asailah ger al-mubashra.

Question marks go at the end of direct questions and sentences that are half statement and half question.
What are you doing today?

Alamat alastfham tuthap fe nahaya al-asailah al-mubashra walgmall alti he nasf bian wensef al-sual.

Exclamation points go at the end of sentences to show emphasis. They can express admiration, alarm, surprise, excitement or even anger.
I can't believe you did that!

Naqqat al-tajib tuthap fe nahaya el-gamal lizhar al-turkiz. micnehm al-tabir an al-ijabe waltenbihe walmfage walethara oa hetti alghedheb.

First, always capitalize the first word in a sentence. Second, capitalize all proper nouns such as people's names, states and countries. Third, be sure to capitalize the days of the week and months.
First word: Tacos are delicious.
Proper nouns: Johnny and Tim went to Japan this summer.
Days of the week and months: This year my birthday falls on the first Tuesday in January

Olace douma rassamla al-kalmah al-awla fe jamala. thanial rassamla doumai al-asmaa al-manasibe methel asmaa al-nas waldol walpledan. thalthal-thalthaltha taked minn rassamla ayam al-asboua wal-ashhar.
